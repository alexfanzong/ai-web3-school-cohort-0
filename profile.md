## Core Thesis

### Programmable Compliance / 可编程合规

Compliance should be treated as an upfront product and protocol design layer, not merely an after-the-fact legal remedy. In AI x Web3 work, this means embedding compliance logic into:

- User flows and onboarding.
- Asset and transaction permissions.
- Jurisdiction-aware disclosure and restriction design.
- Escrow, settlement, and conditional release workflows.
- Agent tool permissions and human review checkpoints.
- Audit logs, evidence trails, and explainable decision records.

The goal is not to let AI autonomously decide legal outcomes, but to make compliance constraints visible, programmable, reviewable, and easier to enforce before risky actions happen.
