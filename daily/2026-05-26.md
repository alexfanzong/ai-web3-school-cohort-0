# Daily Note: 2026-05-26

## Topic

AI x payments: Agentic Wallet vs AI-native payment infrastructure.

## Summary

Today two AI x payment discussions happened on the same day, and reading them side by side was useful. On the Web3 side, Cobo's product lead gave a session on Agentic Wallet and the trust infrastructure needed when AI agents can operate on-chain funds. On the Web2 side, Alipay announced its "支 New Era" full-stack AI-native payment system.

The two routes use very different infrastructure and serve different users, but they converge on the same abstract point: agents should not hold a "naked wallet." They need an authorization layer, a control plane, and an audit mechanism.

## Cobo: Agentic Wallet

The core thesis from Cobo's Agentic Wallet session was that AI has moved from chatbot-style interaction to autonomous agents that can operate on-chain funds, but the trust infrastructure has not caught up. The opening idea was: "the technology is ready, but the trust infrastructure is not."

Two examples made the problem concrete:

- Silent Override: an agent silently changed an amount even though the prompt explicitly prohibited it.
- Shadow Custody: an agent created a wallet outside the MPC setup and bypassed the intended control system.

Both cases point to the same issue: natural language can express intent, but it cannot enforce permissions. A prompt can tell an agent what not to do, but real fund safety cannot rely only on language constraints.

Cobo's proposed structure has three layers:

- MPC for AI Agents: a 2/2 threshold setup where the agent and the human each hold one share, so neither side can move funds alone.
- Pact Authorization Protocol: structured authorization made of Intent, Execution Plan, Policies, and Completion Conditions, enforced at the wallet infrastructure layer.
- Recipe Skill Layer: pre-validated on-chain operation templates that reduce the risk of hallucinated contract addresses or incorrect execution paths.

The line "Don't give Agents Wallets. Give them Pacts." captures the point well. It is not about giving an agent a more powerful wallet. It is about placing the agent inside an action framework that is authorized, constrained, and auditable.

## Alipay: AI-Native Payment

Alipay's "支 New Era" launch focused on embedding payment capabilities into agent-era commerce. Four products stood out:

- AI Pay: users authorize agents to spend, with more than 300 million transactions and a reported loss rate of one in one hundred million.
- AI Receive: merchants can receive purchases initiated by agents and integrate their services as Skills.
- Token Pay: a payment solution for model subscriptions.
- AI Wallet: 24-hour monitoring and intelligent consumption management.

Ant Group CEO Han Xinyi's judgment is worth remembering: the nature of commerce has not changed, but the new role of agents will restructure many relationships. Decision-making is moving from humans to agents. "People looking for services" may become "services looking for people"; product transactions may become task transactions; and two-sided markets may become multi-sided markets.

This shows that Alipay is not only thinking about a single wallet feature. It is thinking about how payment, risk control, merchant onboarding, user authorization, and compensation mechanisms work together once agents enter real commercial networks.

## Route Comparison

Cobo and Alipay represent two different trust routes.

Cobo is closer to the Web3-native route: using cryptography, MPC, smart contracts, and structured authorization to turn trust into infrastructure. It is closer to self-custody and fits crypto-native users, technical users, and high-autonomy scenarios.

Alipay is closer to the Web2 mainstream route: using centralized risk control, payment networks, merchant ecosystems, and a service promise of "you dare to pay, I dare to compensate" to turn trust into a product experience that ordinary users can understand and use. It is closer to custodial infrastructure and fits mainstream consumers and large-scale commerce.

The two routes look very different on the surface, but their problem definition inside the Agent Economy is converging: agents should not directly hold wallets or payment authority. They need to pass through an authorization layer. Cobo calls this Pact. Alipay discussed the ACT protocol as a common language for agent collaboration.

## My Takeaway

My main takeaway is that the bottleneck of the Agent Economy may not be model capability alone. It may be the authorization, constraint, and audit layer between humans and agents.

When an agent is only chatting, mistakes mainly stay at the content layer. But when an agent starts spending money, transferring assets, calling contracts, or placing orders, mistakes enter the real world. At that point, prompts can express intent, but they cannot replace a permission system.

This is highly relevant to Programmable Compliance / 可编程合规. Future compliance should not only judge after the fact whether an action was lawful. It should be embedded earlier into agent workflows, wallet design, payment protocols, and tool-use boundaries. In other words, compliance needs to move from after-the-fact review to an executable control plane.
