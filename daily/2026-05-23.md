# Daily Note: 2026-05-23

## Event

Shanghai Bittensor AI Subnet Ideathon.

## Project

LUO: Legal Uncertainty Oracle, a cross-jurisdiction legal risk topology map.

## Core Positioning

LUO does not output a single legal answer. It maps legal uncertainty and punishes fabricated certainty.

One-line summary:

> Chainlink tells you where the price is; LUO tells you where legal uncertainty is.

## Bittensor Subnet Understanding

Bittensor decomposes AI capability into vertical markets. Each subnet is a market:

- Miners produce intelligence goods.
- Validators audit quality.
- Yuma Consensus aggregates consensus.
- Token incentives distribute rewards.

The key design question is not code volume. It is the definition of the intelligence good and the anti-cheating mechanism.

## LUO Mechanism

- Miners extract evidence from legal corpus.
- Each legal claim must be bound to concrete source documents.
- Validators score along three dimensions:
  - whether citations truly exist;
  - whether the output falls into planted synthetic traps;
  - whether the conclusion exceeds the evidence boundary.

## Demo Case

Tornado Cash: the same protocol has different legal outcomes across the United States, the Netherlands, Switzerland, and Hong Kong. Traditional legal opinions often compress these differences into one conclusion. LUO does the opposite: it expands disagreement into a jurisdictional risk topology.

## Key Lessons

- Miner cheating prevention is not an edge case; it is daily subnet operations.
- The scoring function determines whether a subnet survives.
- Judges care most about intelligence good definition and anti-cheating mechanisms.
