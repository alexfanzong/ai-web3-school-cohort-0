# Daily Note: 2026-05-23

## Event

Shanghai Bittensor AI Subnet Ideathon.

## Summary

Today I joined a full-day Bittensor AI subnet ideathon. The main learning was how a subnet should be framed as an intelligence market rather than a normal app: participants produce intelligence goods, validators evaluate quality, and incentives depend on the scoring mechanism.

## My Takeaway

The most important design lesson is that a subnet lives or dies by the definition of its intelligence good and the robustness of its evaluation mechanism. Code volume matters less than whether the market can reward useful work and resist low-quality or adversarial submissions.

## Project Reflection

I explored a legal/compliance-oriented subnet direction around legal uncertainty. The useful product insight is that legal AI should not only chase confident answers. In cross-jurisdiction contexts, preserving uncertainty and disagreement can be more valuable than compressing everything into a single conclusion.

## Programmable Compliance Connection

This connects to Programmable Compliance because compliance systems should know when not to automate. If the evidence is incomplete, jurisdiction-dependent, or disputed, the product should surface uncertainty and route the decision to human review instead of pretending the answer is settled.
