# Daily Note: 2026-05-22

## Thesis

AI 缺的是可信，Web3 缺的是智能。

## Summary

AI is strong at judgment-like outputs, but weak at trust, provenance, and accountability. Web3 is strong at verification and auditability, but weak at flexible judgment. The meaningful AI x Web3 direction is structural complementarity: put AI judgment into verifiable containers.

## Programmable Compliance Connection

AI Oracle is a key component for Programmable Compliance. Many compliance questions are non-binary:

- Is this evidence authentic?
- Is this transaction suspicious?
- What is the counterparty risk level?
- Which jurisdictional restriction applies?

These cannot be cleanly reduced to if-else logic. But if AI makes the judgment, its output needs provenance, reviewability, and a path into trusted workflows.

## My Takeaway

The stronger thesis is not "AI makes Web3 easier". It is "Web3 makes AI more trustworthy in high-value workflows." That framing fits compliance, RWA, Agentic Commerce, and DAO governance better than generic tooling narratives.

## Follow-Up

- Turn "AI Oracle for Programmable Compliance" into a prototype brief.
- Separate three layers: trustworthy AI output, agent accountability, compliance workflow automation.
