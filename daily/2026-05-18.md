## My Takeaway

The most useful frame from the session was the engineering and security-first approach. Starting from a simple payment system makes Web3 concepts easier to understand because wallet, transaction, signature, simulation, and server-side security can be connected through one concrete workflow instead of treated as isolated terms.

The human-AI collaboration boundary is the key point for my own direction. AI can help with drafting, simulation, explanation, and review, but architecture judgment and high-risk execution boundaries still need explicit human control.

## Programmable Compliance Connection

Programmable Compliance should be designed before the risky action happens:

- before a wallet signs;
- before a payment or settlement executes;
- before an Agent calls a high-risk tool;
- before a legal/compliance conclusion becomes product behavior.

The product should make these boundaries visible, reviewable, and auditable.
