# Daily Note: 2026-05-20

## Topic

Self-custody, public goods funding, privacy boundaries, and fair distribution.

## Summary

Today's notes converge on one pattern: Web3 governance problems become tractable when rights, responsibilities, fees, and review paths are made explicit in mechanism design.

## My Takeaway

Self-custody should not be reduced to "protect the private key". A better framing is recoverable permission architecture: signing rights, recovery rights, routine operation rights, and service-provider responsibilities should be separated.

## Programmable Compliance Connection

Programmable Compliance can appear in four layers:

- Custody: account abstraction, recovery, key roles, and operation limits.
- Fiscal logic: protocol fees, redistribution rules, and public goods funding.
- Boundary compliance: KYT, Travel Rule, compliant routing, and fiat/RWA interfaces.
- Distribution: measurable contribution, verifiable rules, and dispute paths.
