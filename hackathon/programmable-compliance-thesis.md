# Programmable Compliance Thesis

## One-Line Thesis

Programmable Compliance / 可编程合规 means treating compliance as an upfront product and protocol design concern, not merely an after-the-fact legal remedy.

## Why It Matters

AI x Web3 products can move faster than traditional review processes. Agents can read, recommend, route, negotiate, and trigger workflows; Web3 systems can execute permissions, transfers, governance actions, and settlement with durable records.

That combination creates a practical requirement: products should surface risk, uncertainty, permissions, and review boundaries before high-impact actions happen.

## Design Principle

Compliance constraints should be:

- visible to users and operators;
- understandable before execution;
- tied to human review where risk is high;
- recorded enough to support later explanation;
- designed into the workflow rather than patched on afterward.

## Application Areas

- RWA: asset and participant restrictions.
- DAO: proposal context, governance process, and stakeholder impact.
- Agentic Commerce: delegated authority, spending boundaries, and transaction accountability.
- Cross-border settlement: jurisdiction-aware disclosures, evidence review, and dispute handling.

## Non-Goal

The goal is not to let AI autonomously make legal conclusions, approve transfers, or replace regulated professionals. The goal is to make legal/compliance constraints a first-class part of product architecture before irreversible actions happen.

## Publication Boundary

This public note records the thesis. Detailed product mechanics, evaluation logic, scoring systems, and implementation strategy should remain private until intentionally released.
